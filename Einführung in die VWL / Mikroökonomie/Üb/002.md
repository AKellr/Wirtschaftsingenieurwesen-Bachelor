### 1. Mathematical Framework: Utility Maximization (Lagrange)
* **Objective Function (Cobb-Douglas):** Maximize aggregate ordinal utility from two distinct goods.
  $$U(q_1, q_2) = q_1^2 q_2$$
* **Constraint Equation:** The consumption bundle is bound strictly by the nominal budget income ($I$).
  $$I = p_1 q_1 + p_2 q_2 \implies I - p_1 q_1 - p_2 q_2 = 0$$

---

### 2. Operational Calculus & First-Order Conditions (FOC)
* **Lagrangian Functional Setup:** Synthesizing the utility target with the budget boundary using the Lagrange multiplier ($\lambda$).
  $$\mathcal{L}(q_1, q_2, \lambda) = q_1^2 q_2 + \lambda(I - p_1 q_1 - p_2 q_2)$$
* **Partial Differentiation Vector:**
  1. $\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial q_1} = 2q_1 q_2 - \lambda p_1 = 0 \implies 2q_1 q_2 = \lambda p_1$
  2. $\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial q_2} = q_1^2 - \lambda p_2 = 0 \implies q_1^2 = \lambda p_2$
  3. $\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = I - p_1 q_1 - p_2 q_2 = 0$

---

### 3. Structural Elimination & Marginal Rate of Substitution
* **Ratio System Isolation:** Dividing the first partial derivative equation by the second cancels out the internal pricing shadow scale factor ($\lambda$).
  $$\frac{2q_1 q_2}{q_1^2} = \frac{\lambda p_1}{\lambda p_2} \implies \frac{2q_2}{q_1} = \frac{p_1}{p_2}$$
* **Economic Identity (Optimum Condition):** The Marginal Rate of Substitution ($\text{MRS}$) is equated directly to the relative price ratio.
  $$\text{MRS} = \frac{MU_1}{MU_2} = \frac{2q_2}{q_1} = \frac{p_1}{p_2}$$
* **Factor Substitution Substitution:** Cross-multiplying yields the precise monetary structural value allocation for good 2:
  $$p_2 q_2 = \frac{p_1 q_1}{2}$$

---

### 4. Final Demand Functions Derivation
* **Univariate Budget Reduction:** Injecting the factor substitution rule back directly into the initial linear constraint equation eliminates $q_2$:
  $$I = p_1 q_1 + \frac{p_1 q_1}{2} \implies I = \frac{3}{2} p_1 q_1$$
* **Marshallian Demand Vector Equations:**
  * **Optimal Allocation $q_1^*$:** Fixed income share coefficient expenditure of exactly $\frac{2}{3}$ of total budget allocation.
    $$q_1^* = \frac{2I}{3p_1}$$
  * **Optimal Allocation $q_2^*$:** Found by re-substituting $q_1^*$ back into the isolated factor value formula, consuming $\frac{1}{3}$ of nominal wealth.
    $$q_2^* = \frac{I}{3p_2}$$

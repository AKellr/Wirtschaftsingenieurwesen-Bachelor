### 1. Mathematical Framework: Cost Minimization (Lagrangian)
* **Objective Function (Linear Total Cost):** Minimize nominal expenditure on production inputs Labor ($L$) and Capital ($K$) given factor prices wage ($w$) and interest rate ($r$).
  $$C(L, K) = wL + rK$$
* **Constraint Equation:** Output must precisely satisfy a predetermined target production volume ($\bar{q}$).
  $$\bar{q} = 3LK \implies \bar{q} - 3LK = 0$$

---

### 2. Operational Calculus & First-Order Conditions (FOC)
* **Lagrangian Functional Setup:** Combining the cost minimization objective with the operational output ceiling via the Lagrange multiplier ($\lambda$).
  $$\mathcal{L}(L, K, \lambda) = wL + rK + \lambda(\bar{q} - 3LK)$$
* **Partial Differentiation Vector:**
  1. $\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial L} = w - 3\lambda K = 0 \implies w = 3\lambda K$
  2. $\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial K} = r - 3\lambda L = 0 \implies r = 3\lambda L$
  3. $\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = \bar{q} - 3LK = 0$

---

### 3. Structural Elimination & Technical Substitution
* **Ratio System Isolation:** Dividing the two factor-price equations eliminates the structural shadow multiplier ($\lambda$).
  $$\frac{w}{r} = \frac{3\lambda K}{3\lambda L} \implies \frac{w}{r} = \frac{K}{L}$$
* **Economic Identity (Optimum Condition):** The Marginal Rate of Technical Substitution ($\text{MRTS}$) is equilibrated to the factor price ratio.
  $$\text{MRTS} = \frac{MP_L}{MP_K} = \frac{3K}{3L} = \frac{K}{L} = \frac{w}{r}$$
* **Factor Substitution Trajectory:** Isolating Capital ($K$) yields the expansion path equation for optimal input scaling:
  $$K = \frac{w}{r}L$$

---

### 4. Conditional Factor Demand Functions Derivation
* **Univariate Isoquant Reduction:** Injecting the expansion path expression into the target production function systematically isolates $L$:
  $$\bar{q} = 3L\left(\frac{w}{r}L\right) \implies \bar{q} = \frac{3wL^2}{r}$$
* **Hicksian / Conditional Factor Demands:**
  * **Optimal Labor Demand $L^*$:**
    $$L^*(w, r, \bar{q}) = \sqrt{\frac{\bar{q} \cdot r}{3w}}$$
  * **Optimal Capital Demand $K^*$:** Derived by re-substituting $L^*$ back into the optimal expansion path identity.
    $$K^*(w, r, \bar{q}) = \sqrt{\frac{w \cdot \bar{q}}{3r}}$$

---

### 5. Scale Elasticity Analysis (Returns to Scale)
* **Scaling Experiment:** Evaluating the production metric behavior under a globally uniform input amplification scalar $t > 1$.
  $$f(tL, tK) = 3(tL)(tK) = t^2 \cdot (3LK) = t^2 \cdot f(L, K)$$
* **Economic Interpretation:** Because the structural scaling factor possesses a degree of homogeneity $\alpha = 2 > 1$, the underlying system exhibits **Increasing Returns to Scale (Zunehmende Skalenerträge)**. Doubling all resource inputs yields a non-linear, over-proportional doubling of net physical output volume.

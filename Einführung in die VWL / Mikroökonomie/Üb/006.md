### 1. Game Theoretic Classification
* **Duopoly Model:** The scenario in `image_c4183b.png` describes a non-cooperative market containing exactly two firms operating under sequential quantity competition.
* **Stackelberg Framework:** This structural form is designated as a **Stackelberg-Duopol** (or Stackelberg competition). The game is played sequentially via non-cooperative actions across two distinct strategic tiers:
  * **The Leader (👑 Marktführer):** Firm 1, which commands strategic market power and commits to an output quantity first.
  * **The Follower (💼 Folger):** Firm 2, which observes the leader's finalized commitment and optimizes its own quantity sequentially.

---

### 2. Operational Parameters & Assumptions
* **Inverse Demand Function:** The aggregate market boundary is parameterized by the linear price function:
  $$p(x) = 1200 - 3x$$
* **Output Symmetries:** Total market clearing quantity ($x$) is the scalar sum of the individual firm choices:
  $$x = x_1 + x_2$$
* **Cost Structure:** Both firms operate with symmetric, zero marginal cost curves:
  $$K'(x_1) = K'(x_2) = 0$$

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Analytical Derivation

#### Step 1: Follower Optimization via Backward Induction
The game is solved using backward induction. We model the follower’s reaction to an arbitrary leader volume $x_1$. The profit function of the follower ($\Pi_2$) corresponds purely to revenue since marginal costs are zero:
$$\Pi_2 = p(x) \cdot x_2 = (1200 - 3x_1 - 3x_2) \cdot x_2 = 1200x_2 - 3x_1x_2 - 3x_2^2$$

Taking the first-order partial derivative with respect to $x_2$ and setting it to zero yields:
$$\frac{\partial \Pi_2}{\partial x_2} = 1200 - 3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0$$

Solving for $x_2$ yields the follower’s **Best-Response Function (Reaktionsfunktion)**:
$$x_2(x_1) = \frac{1200 - 3x_1}{6} = 200 - 0.5x_1$$

#### Step 2: Leader Optimization incorporating Strategic Anticipation
The leader anticipates the follower’s operational response and substitutes $x_2(x_1)$ directly into the market clearing price function:
$$p = 1200 - 3x_1 - 3(200 - 0.5x_1) = 1200 - 3x_1 - 600 + 1.5x_1 = 600 - 1.5x_1$$

The leader's objective function ($\Pi_1$) becomes a function of $x_1$ alone:
$$\Pi_1 = p \cdot x_1 = (600 - 1.5x_1) \cdot x_1 = 600x_1 - 1.5x_1^2$$

Maximizing via the first-order condition:
$$\frac{\partial \Pi_1}{\partial x_1} = 600 - 3x_1 = 0 \implies x_1^* = 200$$

#### Step 3: Backward Substitution for Equilibrium Values
Substituting $x_1^* = 200$ back into the follower's best-response function yields:
$$x_2^* = 200 - 0.5(200) = 100$$

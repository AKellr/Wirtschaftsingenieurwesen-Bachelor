# 1

### Phase 1: Core Theory and Legal Frameworks

Before memorizing booking records, ensure you understand the legal thresholds and foundational concepts.

- **Buchführungspflicht (Accounting Obligations):** Know the difference between commercial law (HGB) and tax law (AO) obligations. Memorize the limits from § 241a HGB: €800,000 in revenue or €80,000 in profit.
    
- **Rechengrößen (Accounting Metrics):** You must be able to distinguish between _Einzahlung/Auszahlung_ (cash flow), _Einnahme/Ausgabe_ (financial assets), _Ertrag/Aufwand_ (P&L), and _Erlös/Kosten_ (cost accounting).
    
- **Inventur (Inventory Methods):** Understand the four methods: _Stichtagsinventur_ (near the reporting date), _zeitverschobene Inventur_ (shifted time), _permanente Inventur_ (continuous), and _Stichprobeninventur_ (statistical sampling).
    

### Phase 2: Master the High-Yield Booking Mechanics

This course emphasizes specific, complex booking scenarios. Prioritize practicing the _Buchungssätze_ (booking entries) for the following:

- **Anschaffungskosten (Acquisition Costs):** Practice calculating these by adding the purchase price and incidental acquisition costs (e.g., notary, land transfer tax, transport), and subtracting reductions like discounts. **Crucial trap:** Financing costs (like loan fees) are strictly excluded from _Anschaffungskosten_.
    
- **Umsatzsteuer (Value Added Tax):** Practice booking incoming invoices with _Vorsteuer_ (input tax) and outgoing invoices with _Umsatzsteuer_ (output tax). Ensure you know how to calculate the _Zahllast_ (tax liability to be paid to the tax office).
    
- **Skonti (Discounts):** Practice the specific entries for _erhaltene Skonti_ (received) and _gewährte Skonti_ (granted), ensuring you also correct the corresponding _Vorsteuer_ or _Umsatzsteuer_.
    
- **Bruttolohnverbuchung (Payroll):** This is highly mechanical and heavily tested. Practice the chain of booking gross wages, deducting wage tax (_LSt_), church tax (_KiSt_), and the employee/employer portions of social security (_SV-Beiträge_) using a _Lohnverrechnungskonto_ (payroll clearing account).
    
- **Privatentnahmen (Private Withdrawals):** Learn the exact entries for cash withdrawals, goods withdrawals, and usage withdrawals. Pay special attention to the 1% rule (_1%-Regelung_) for the private use of company cars, including the non-deductible expenses for commutes.
    

### Phase 3: Year-End Adjustments (Jahresabschluss)

The final segment of the course focuses on bringing the books to a close.

- **Abschreibungen (Depreciation):** Practice linear depreciation, particularly _pro-rata temporis_ (monthly precision) for assets bought mid-year.
    
- **Geringwertige Wirtschaftsgüter (GwG):** Memorize the tax thresholds for low-value assets: immediate write-off up to €250, immediate write-off up to €800, or the pool depreciation method (_Sammelposten_) for assets between €250 and €1,000 spread over 5 years.
    
- **Rechnungsabgrenzungsposten (RAP):** You must be able to separate transitorisch (payment now, expense/income later; use ARAP/PRAP) from antizipativ (expense/income now, payment later; use _sonstige Forderungen/Verbindlichkeiten_).
    
- **Forderungsbewertung (Valuation of Receivables):** Practice adjusting doubtful accounts. Remember to adjust the net amount for _Einzelwertberichtigungen_ (EWB). Only correct the VAT once the debt is definitively uncollectible (_uneinbringlich_). Also, review how to calculate a _Pauschalwertberichtigung_ (PWB) for general credit risk.
    

### Suggested Practice Questions to Test Yourself

To gauge your readiness, try answering these questions based on the script:

1. **Theory:** A small business owner reaches €850,000 in revenue but only €75,000 in profit. Are they required to keep double-entry books under HGB? What about under AO?
    
2. **Asset Acquisition:** A company buys a machine for €100,000 (net). Transport is €2,000 (net), and a bank loan fee to finance the machine is €1,500. What is the exact value activated on the balance sheet?
    
3. **Payroll:** Formulate the booking entries for an employee earning €3,000 gross, with €600 in taxes, €600 in employee social security, and €600 in employer social security.
    
4. **Year-End:** On October 1st, a company pays €1,200 for a 12-month insurance policy covering October 1st through September 30th of the following year. Formulate the booking entry for the payment and the entry required on December 31st.


## Core System Variables
*   **Demand Function ($q_D$):** $q_D = 50 - 2p \implies p(q_D) = 25 - 0.5q_D$
*   **Supply Function ($q_S$):** $q_S = 10 + 3p \implies p(q_S) = \frac{q_S - 10}{3}$
*   **Market Equilibrium (Reference):** $q^* = 40$, $p^* = 5$
*   **Price Ceiling ($p_{max}$):** $6$

---

## Technical Overview & Effects

### 1. Quantity Determination (Task 2.1)
Substituting $p_{max} = 6$ into the market functions:
*   **Quantity Demanded ($q_D$):** 
    $$q_D = 50 - 2(6) = 38$$
*   **Quantity Supplied ($q_S$):** 
    $$q_S = 10 + 3(6) = 28$$

### 2. Market Imbalance (Task 2.2)
Since $q_D > q_S$, the binding constraint is determined by the short side of the market ($q_{actual} = q_S = 28$).
*   **Phenomenon:** Nachfrageüberschuss (Excess Demand / Shortage)
*   **Quantification:** 
    $$\Delta q = q_D - q_S = 38 - 28 = 10 \text{ units}$$

### 3. Welfare Effects (Task 2.3)
To compute consumer surplus, the marginal willingness to pay at the restricted quantity ($q = 28$) is derived via the inverse demand function:
$$p(28) = 25 - 0.5(28) = 11$$

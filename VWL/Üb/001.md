### 1. Market Regulation & Price Ceilings (Höchstpreis)
* **Context Reference:** *Handout_L2.pdf* (Slides 16, 18, 19) & *Mock_SS26_Lsg.pdf* (Aufgabe 6).
* **Binding Condition:** A price ceiling ($p_{\max}$) is binding if and only if it is set strictly *below* the unregulated market equilibrium price ($p_{\max} < p^*$).

---

### 2. Market Dynamics & Shortage
* **Quantity Distortions:** Lowering the price artificially causes an asymmetry between market participants:
  * **Consumers:** Quantity demanded ($q_D$) increases because the good is cheaper.
  * **Producers:** Quantity supplied ($q_S$) decreases because production becomes less profitable.
* **Market Shortage:** The divergence leads to a supply deficit or demand surplus (Nachfrageüberhang):
  $$\text{Nachfrageüberhang} = q_D(p_{\max}) - q_S(p_{\max}) > 0$$
* **Short-Side Rule:** Total volume traded is restricted by the shorter side of the market, meaning actual transactions drop to $q_S(p_{\max})$.

---

### 3. Graphical Layout & Welfare Shifts
* **Axis Configuration:** Price ($p$) on the vertical axis, quantity ($q$) on the horizontal axis.
* **Welfare Allocation Changes:**
  * **Consumer Surplus Shift:** Consumers win area $a$ (paying a lower price) but lose area $b$ (reduced market volume). 
    $$\Delta CS = +a - b$$
  * **Producer Surplus Shift:** Producers lose area $a$ to consumers and area $c$ to market contraction.
    $$\Delta PS = -a - c$$
  * **Deadweight Loss ($DWL$):** The net aggregate societal loss from the quantity restriction ($q^* \rightarrow q_S$). It forms a geometric triangle pointing towards the old market equilibrium:
    $$DWL = b + c$$

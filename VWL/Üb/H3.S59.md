### 1. Temporal Horizon & Structural Breakdown
* **Kurze Frist (Short-Run):** The cost model operates under short-run constraints due to the existence of fixed operational costs ($FC = 200 \neq 0$). 
* **Cost Function Properties:** Total production costs ($C$) are structurally parameterized by static fixed assets and quadratically scaling variable inputs:
  $$C(q) = 200 + 2q^2$$

---

### 2. Operational Conditions for Profit Maximization
* **Market Symmetries:** Under perfect competition, individual enterprises act as price takers, facing a horizontal demand curve where market price ($p$) equals marginal revenue ($MR$).
* **Optimality Condition:** Net profit is maximized when the incremental revenue of the last unit sold matches its incremental production cost. This yields the standard competitive equilibrium condition:
  $$p = MC$$

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Equilibrium Solution
Given a fixed exogenous market price of $p = \$100$:

#### Step 1: Analytical Derivation of Marginal Costs
The marginal cost function ($MC$) is derived via the first-order partial derivative of the total cost function ($C$) with respect to output ($q$):
$$MC(q) = \frac{\partial C}{\partial q} = \frac{\partial (200 + 2q^2)}{\partial q} = 4q$$

#### Step 2: Optimal Output Optimization
Equating the market price to the derived marginal cost function determines the profit-maximizing output quantity ($q^*$):
$$100 = 4q \implies q^* = 25 \text{ units}$$

#### Step 3: Financial Balance & Net Profit Evaluation
Profit ($\pi$) is calculated by establishing the financial vector difference between aggregate revenue ($R$) and total short-run operational costs ($C$):

* **Aggregate Revenue ($R$):**
  $$R(25) = p \cdot q^* = 100 \cdot 25 = 2500$$
* **Total Short-Run Costs ($C$):**
  $$C(25) = 200 + 2(25)^2 = 200 + 2(625) = 1450$$
* **Net Corporate Profit ($\pi$):**
  $$\pi = 2500 - 1450 = 1050$$

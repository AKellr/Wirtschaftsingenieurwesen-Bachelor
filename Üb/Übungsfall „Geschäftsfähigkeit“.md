# Fall
Tante T schenkt dem 17jährigen M 1000 € zur Anschaffung eines großen Lexikons. Die Eltern sind mit der Anschaffung des Lexikons einverstanden. M kauft stattdessen aber mit dem Geld bei V ein gebrauchtes Mofa zum Preis von 900 €. Die Eltern sind empört und lehnen das Geschäft ab.

Kann V von M die Zahlung des Kaufpreises verlangen?

# Losung
(Who wants what from whom based on what?).
- **Wer (Who):** Verkäufer V (Seller)
- **Was (What):** Zahlung des Kaufpreises in Höhe von 900 € (Payment of 900 €)
- **Wem (Whom):** Minderjähriger M (Minor)
- **Woraus (Legal basis):** Kaufvertrag gem. § 433 Abs. 2 BGB (Purchase agreement)

Here is the breakdown of the legal analysis:
### 1. Obersatz (Hypothesis)
V könnte gegen M einen Anspruch auf Zahlung des Kaufpreises in Höhe von 900 € aus § 433 Abs. 2 BGB haben.

### 2. Anspruch entstanden? (Did the claim arise?)
For this claim to exist, a valid purchase agreement (Kaufvertrag) must have been concluded between V and M. A contract requires two matching declarations of intent: an offer and an acceptance. M and V agreed on the purchase of the moped, so a basic agreement exists.
### 3. Wirksamkeit der Einigung (Validity of the agreement)
The core issue of this case is whether M's declaration of intent is legally binding, given his age.
- **Beschränkte Geschäftsfähigkeit (§ 106 BGB):** Since M is 17 years old, he is considered a minor (Minderjähriger) and has restricted legal capacity (beschränkte Geschäftsfähigkeit) according to §§ 2, 106 BGB. The validity of his declarations depends on §§ 107-111 BGB.
    
- **Lediglich rechtlicher Vorteil (§ 107 BGB)?** We must check if the purchase brings M "only a legal advantage" (lediglich rechtlicher Vorteil). A purchase agreement obligates M to pay the purchase price (§ 433 II BGB), which is a legal disadvantage. Therefore, M cannot conclude this contract completely on his own.
    
- **Einwilligung (§ 107 BGB)?** Since the contract is legally disadvantageous, M needs the prior consent (Einwilligung) of his legal representatives (his parents). The parents only consented to the purchase of an encyclopedia, not a moped. Therefore, there is no prior consent for this specific transaction.
    
- **"Taschengeldparagraph" (§ 110 BGB)?** A contract can still be valid if the minor effects the performance with means provided by the legal representative for a _specific purpose_ or for _free disposal_. Here, Aunt T gave the 1000 € (with the parents' consent) specifically for the _purpose of buying an encyclopedia_. Because the money was earmarked for a specific purpose and not given "for free disposal" (zur freien Verfügung) for whatever M wanted, § 110 BGB does not apply.

### 4. Schwebende Unwirksamkeit und Ergebnis (§ 108 BGB)
Because the contract was not legally advantageous, lacked prior consent, and does not fall under the pocket money rule, the contract is initially "pending invalid" (schwebend unwirksam) according to § 108 Abs. 1 BGB.

Its final validity depends on the subsequent approval (Genehmigung) of the parents. According to the case facts, the parents are outraged and reject the transaction. Because the parents refused their approval, the contract becomes permanently invalid (endgültig unwirksam).

**Conclusion (Ergebnis):** Since no valid contract exists, V cannot demand payment of the 900 € from M.

> Da die Eltern die Genehmigung verweigert haben, ist der Vertrag nach § 108 Abs. 1 BGB endgültig unwirksam. V hat folglich gegen M keinen Anspruch auf Zahlung des Kaufpreises in Höhe von 900 € gemäß § 433 Abs. 2 BGB.

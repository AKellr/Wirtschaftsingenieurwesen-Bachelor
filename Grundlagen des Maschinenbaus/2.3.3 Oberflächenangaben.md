## 1. Introduction

Technical drawings represent the **ideal geometry** _(Idealgeometrie)_ of a part.  
In reality, manufactured parts always show **form deviations** _(Gestaltabweichungen)_ caused by machining processes, material properties, and production tolerances.  
Decisions about the permissible deviations are part of the **design process** _(Konstruktionsprozess)_.

---

## 2. Form Deviations _(Gestaltabweichungen)_

Kap. 2.3.3 Oberflachenangaben

A manufactured surface never matches the mathematically perfect model. Deviations can occur in:

- **Form** _(Form)_
    
- **Waviness** _(Welligkeit)_
    
- **Roughness** _(Rauheit)_
    

These deviations are categorized to ensure functional requirements such as sealing, fit, friction, and fatigue resistance are met.

---

## 3. Surface Parameters _(Oberflächenkenngrößen)_

Kap. 2.3.3 Oberflachenangaben

Surface parameters describe the texture and quality of a surface. Common quantities include:

### **Arithmetic Mean Roughness $R_a$**

$(\text{Mittenrauwert } R_a)$

- Average deviation from the mean line.
    

### **Maximum Roughness Depth $R_z$**

$(\text{Gemittelte Rautiefe } R_z)$

- Average of the vertical distance between highest peaks and lowest valleys over several sampling lengths.
    

### **Peak Count $RP_c$**

$(\text{Gipfelanzahl } RP_c)$

- Number of significant peaks per unit length.
    

### **Surface Profile**

$(\text{Oberflächenprofil})$

- Graphical representation of the measured surface deviation.
    

These values define the allowed surface quality and influence manufacturing processes and costs.

## 4. Surface Symbols _(Oberflächensymbole)_

Kap. 2.3.3 Oberflachenangaben

Specifications for surface symbols follow **DIN EN ISO 1302**.

### **Basic Symbol**

The base symbol is a **checkmark-like shape** used to indicate that a surface requirement exists.

### **Variants**

- **Machining required** _(Materialabtrag erforderlich)_
    
- **No material removal allowed** _(Materialabtrag unzulässig)_
    
- **Free choice of production method** _(Fertigungsverfahren frei wählbar)_
    

### **Additional Information**

Symbols may include:

- Roughness values (e.g., $R_a = 3.2,\mu m$)
    
- Production methods (e.g., **ground** _(geschliffen)_, **turned** _(gedreht)_)
    
- Notes on surface lay _(Richtungsangabe der Oberflächenstruktur)_
    

---

## 5. Placement Rules _(Eintragung der Oberflächenangaben)_

Kap. 2.3.3 Oberflachenangaben

- Symbols are usually added **only to functional surfaces** _(Funktionsflächen)_.
    
- The symbol is placed **directly on the surface** or connected via a **leader line with arrow** _(Hinweisslinie mit Maßpfeil)_.
    
- Symbols must be readable **from the right or from below** _(von rechts oder unten lesbar)_.
    
- Surface information should be shown **in the same view** where the relevant dimension is given.
    

---

## 6. Examples _(Beispiele mit Oberflächenkenngröße)_

Kap. 2.3.3 Oberflachenangaben

Examples often show:

- Symbols with a specific roughness requirement
    
- Combined symbols (e.g., roughness + manufacturing process)
    
- Placement on multiple surfaces or edges
    

Common example:

   `⌒   3.2`

Meaning: $R_a = 3.2,\mu m$, surface machining required.

## 7. Edge Condition _(Kantenzustand)_

Kap. 2.3.3 Oberflachenangaben

Edge condition describes the **quality of edges whose exact geometry is not defined**.

### Cases:

- **Undefined edges** _(Kanten unbestimmter Form)_  
    → May receive a general edge treatment (e.g., "edges broken" _(Kanten gebrochen)_).
    
- **Defined edges** _(Kanten bestimmter Form)_ – such as bevels or chamfers _(Fase)_  
    → These must be **dimensioned explicitly** (e.g., $C1.0$ for a 1 mm chamfer).
    

The **edge condition symbol** ensures consistent manufacturing quality and eliminates ambiguity in part production.
